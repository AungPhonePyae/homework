About this book

    The goal of this book is to teach you think like software developer (engineer) or computer scientist.
Like mathematicians, software developers use formal languages to denote ideas intern of computational.
Like engineers, they design things, assembling componets into systems and evaluating tradeoffs among alternatives.
Like scientists, computer scientists observe the behavior of the complex system, from hypothesis and test predictions.

    The most imprtant skill for a computer scientist is problem-solving, ability to formulate problems,
think creatively about solutions and finally express a solution clearly and accurately.
Study shows that learning how to program is an excellent skill to practice problem-solving skills.

Target audience

  • Have little or no experience at programming academic students
